Controls that actually make sense

  • Color Picker & Multi-Color Selection: Sample any pixel on the image to set a target color. Add multiple targets to remove complex, multi-toned backgrounds simultaneously.
  • Similarity slider: Controls the tolerance for color matching. Increase the value to remove gradients or shadows; decrease it for strict, single-hex-code precision.
  • Softness & Spill Removal: Analyzes semi-transparent border pixels to separate the foreground subject from background bleed. Crucial for hair, fur, or soft-focus edges.
  • Edge-only mode: Restricts the removal algorithm to pixels touching the outside border of the image, protecting internal subject colors.

How to remove a background color — step by step

  1. Drop or choose a PNG/JPG image.
  2. Click the color in the preview, or pick it with the color box.
  3. Adjust Similarity to control how close a color must be to get removed.
  4. Adjust Softness for smart-edge blending — partial-coverage border pixels get proportional transparency and spill removal, so edges stay smooth with no colored halo.
  5. Enable Remove edge-connected only to delete the outside background while keeping the same color inside the subject.
  6. Use the 🪄 Wand mode to erase just one connected region by clicking it.
  7. Download the transparent PNG.

Questions worth answering

How do I make a logo background transparent without Photoshop?

Upload your JPG or PNG logo. Click the background color (usually white or black) using the color picker. Adjust the Similarity slider until the background disappears, then click Download.

Why is there a white (or colored) outline around my cutout?

White or colored outlines (halos) occur due to anti-aliasing in the original image. To fix this, increase the Softness slider. This activates our spill-removal algorithm, which calculates the true color of edge pixels and blends them smoothly.

Is this safe to use with confidential or client images?

Yes. Unlike cloud-based AI tools, this application is a static HTML file that runs JavaScript locally on your device. It does not send network requests or store images on an external server. You can disconnect your internet and the tool will still function.

How do I erase the background without removing white text or a logo?

Enable the Remove edge-connected only toggle, or use the Wand mode. This ensures the tool only deletes the white pixels touching the outer edge of the image, leaving enclosed white elements (like text or shirt graphics) entirely intact.

Is this really free — or is there a catch?

Yes — completely free with no limits, no sign-up, and no paywall. All processing runs in your browser using client-side JavaScript. You can also download the tool as a single HTML file and run it offline.

Do my images get sent to a server?

No. All image processing happens entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Your images never leave your device and are never sent to any server.

What file formats are supported?

The tool accepts PNG and JPG/JPEG images. The downloaded output is always a transparent PNG.

How do I remove a white background from a PNG?

Upload your PNG, then click the white area in the image preview to sample the color (or manually set the color picker to white). Raise the Similarity slider until all shades of white are captured. The Softness slider smooths the edges so there's no hard cutout line. Hit Download PNG to save.

Can I remove a green screen background?

Yes. Upload your green screen image and click the green area to pick the chroma key color. Raise Similarity to cover all hues of green, and use Softness to handle edge fringing for a smooth, halo-free cutout.

What does Wand mode actually do differently?

Wand mode removes only the connected region you click — like a magic wand selection. This is useful when you want to erase the background but keep interior areas of the same color, for example removing a white background while preserving a white shirt or logo.

Can I target more than one color at a time?

Yes. Use the + Add color button to queue up multiple colors. Each added color becomes a chip you can manage independently. You can also mix Color and Wand targets in the same pass.

The edges still look tinted. How do I fix it?

Semi-transparent edge pixels contain a mix of the foreground and background color. Raise the Softness slider — it activates the Blur-Fusion spill-removal algorithm, which unmixes the edge pixels and recovers the true foreground color, eliminating the colored halo.
